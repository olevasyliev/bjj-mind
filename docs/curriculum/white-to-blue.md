## How to read this file

Each technique includes:
- **Category** — positional context
- **Role** — are you on top (attacking) or bottom (defending/escaping)?
- **Tags** — app concept tags used for questions and unit grouping
- **Key cue** — the most important single coaching point
- **Common mistake** — what white belts typically do wrong
- **Related** — techniques that chain or counter

App tags used: `frames` `escapes` `grips` `timing` `sweeps` `submissions` `control` `takedowns` `self-defense` `base` `transitions`

## APP UNIT MAPPING (White Belt, Stripe 1-4)

Based on difficulty and concept grouping, suggested unit structure:

### Stripe 1 — Survival (Free tier)
- **Unit 1: Intro to Survival** — F2 shrimp, F5 Upa, F7 four points base, F8 stand up in base
- **Unit 2: Frames** — 31 regain guard, 48 elbow escape, 45 mount prevention, 27 front bear hug frame
- **Unit 3: Grip Basics** — 11/12 wrist grabs, 49 maintain mount, 38 maintain cross body, 55 back control
- **Belt Test: Stripe 1** — concepts above

### Stripe 2 — Escapes
- **Unit 4: Escapes from Bottom** — 32 turn to knees, 33-37 side headlock series, 53 back escape
- **Unit 5: Mount Escapes** — 46 mounted punch upa, 47 upa with choke, 48 elbow escape detail
- **Unit 6: Timing & Reactions** — 34 bridge & roll timing, 60/61 choke defenses, 19/20 standing choke defenses
- **Belt Test: Stripe 2** — escapes + timing

### Stripe 3 — Attacks from Top
- **Unit 7: Side Control Attacks** — 39 americana, 40 kimura, 41 neck crank, 42 straight armlock
- **Unit 8: Mount Attacks** — 50 cross choke, 51 americana from mount, 52 armbar
- **Unit 9: Back Attacks** — 56 collar choke, 57 mata leao, 55 maintain back
- **Belt Test: Stripe 3** — attacks from dominant positions

### Stripe 4 — Guard Game
- **Unit 10: Guard Attacks** — 63 cross choke, 65 triangle, 64 armbar, 66 kimura
- **Unit 11: Guard Sweeps** — 70 scissor, 71 hook, 72 sit-up sweep
- **Unit 12: Guard Passing** — 58 posture, 68 kneeling pass, 69 standing pass
- **Belt Test: Stripe 4 / Blue Belt Test** — full curriculum

### Stripe criteria (BJJFanatics reference)
Useful for app unit gating logic:

**Stripe 1:** Upa, Elbow Escape, basic positions, Americana, Hip Bump Sweep, basic guard break
**Stripe 2:** Guard passing (knee cut), Knee on Belly, Scissor Sweep, Guillotine + defense, Back control + RNC, Technical stand-up
**Stripe 3:** Tight control, Open/Closed Guard, Toreando Pass, Cross Collar Choke, Armbar from guard + mount, Armbar defense
**Stripe 4:** Stand-up + single/double leg, Bull Pass, Bow & Arrow Choke, Kimura + Kimura Sweep, Transitions, Triangle

### Blue Belt requirements summary (general)
- At least 1 mastered submission + 1 mastered sweep
- Escape from ALL major positions (mount, side control, guard, back)
- Good control: crossface, underhook, overhook, necktie
- Decent guard pass + posture in guard
- Some stand-up competence

## MINDSET PRINCIPLES (for Coach Moments)
> Source: BJJFanatics.com

| Principle | Quote/Concept | Coach Moment use |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Forget the strength | "Calmness and body positioning far outweighs tensing strength. A rigid student is easier to sweep." | After any escape/sweep question |
| Don't be a YouTube warrior | "White belts who focus on berimbolos miss guard passing, escaping, and transitioning fundamentals." | After first session / unit intro |
| Master one at a time | "Mastering one move at a time is more conceivable than biting off more than you can chew." | After completing a unit |
| Flow rolls | "Explore multiple positions without fighting for survival — you understand positions better when not fighting for your life." | After belt test intro |
| Fail = learn | "In Jiu Jitsu to fail is not to fail — it is simply an opportunity to learn." | After wrong answer feedback |
| Embrace the belt | "When promoted, whether you feel you deserve it or not — step up to that level." | After belt test pass screen |
